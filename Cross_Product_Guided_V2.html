Guided Lesson: Vector Cross Product
Estimated time: 5–7 minutes
Vectron idea: Do not start by memorizing a formula. Start by asking: What kind of quantity is this? What determines its size? What determines its direction?
Step 1. If you compute \(\vec{A}\times\vec{B}\), what kind of quantity do you get?
Not quite. A dot product gives a scalar. The cross product behaves differently.
Correct. The cross product gives a vector.
Not quite. Its numerical value depends on the vectors, but its type is always the same.
Good. So the cross product is not just a number. We must think about both magnitude and direction.
Step 2. What determines the magnitude of \(\vec{A}\times\vec{B}\)?
Think about two parallel vectors. Their cross product should vanish. Which trig function becomes zero for parallel vectors?
No. The magnitude is not the sum of the magnitudes.
Correct. The magnitude is
\(|\vec{A}\times\vec{B}| = |\vec{A}||\vec{B}|\sin\theta\)
That would match the dot product geometry, not the cross product.
Step 3. When is the magnitude of \(\vec{A}\times\vec{B}\) largest?
No. Parallel vectors give zero cross product.
Correct. Since \(\sin\theta\) is largest at \(90^\circ\), the cross product is largest when the vectors are perpendicular.
No. Anti-parallel vectors also give zero cross product.
Step 4. What determines the direction of \(\vec{A}\times\vec{B}\)?
The cross product creates a new vector that is not in the same plane as the original two.
No. It does not point along either original vector.
No. That would still lie in the original plane.
Correct. The direction is perpendicular to the plane of \(\vec{A}\) and \(\vec{B}\), chosen by the right-hand rule.

Right-hand rule

Point your fingers along \(\vec{A}\). Curl them toward \(\vec{B}\) through the smaller angle. Your thumb points in the direction of \(\vec{A}\times\vec{B}\).

Step 5. If \(\hat{i}\times\hat{j}=\hat{k}\), then what is \(\hat{j}\times\hat{i}\)?
Not quite. Reversing the order changes the sign.
Correct. Cross products are anti-commutative:
\(\vec{A}\times\vec{B}=-(\vec{B}\times\vec{A})\)
No. The result is not zero just because the order changed.

Summary

\(\vec{A}\times\vec{B}=|\vec{A}||\vec{B}|\sin\theta\,\hat{n}\)
  • The result is a vector.
  • Its magnitude depends on \(\sin\theta\).
  • Its direction is perpendicular to the plane of the two vectors.
  • The right-hand rule chooses the direction.
  • Reversing the order reverses the sign.

Next step

In the magnetic force law, \(\vec{F}=q\,\vec{v}\times\vec{B}\), the cross product is exactly what determines why the magnetic force is perpendicular to both \(\vec{v}\) and \(\vec{B}\).

✓ Lesson Complete

You have now reviewed the key ideas of the vector cross product:

  • The cross product produces a vector.
  • Its magnitude depends on \( |\vec{A}| |\vec{B}| \sin\theta \).
  • The direction is perpendicular to the plane of the two vectors.
  • The right-hand rule determines the direction.

You are now ready to understand the magnetic force law.
