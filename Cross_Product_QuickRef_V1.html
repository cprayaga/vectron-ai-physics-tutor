Vector Cross Product — Quick Reference

Definition (magnitude + direction)

\(\vec{A}\times\vec{B} = |\vec{A}|\,|\vec{B}|\,\sin\theta\;\hat{n}\)
  • \(\theta\) is the angle between \(\vec{A}\) and \(\vec{B}\).
  • \(\hat{n}\) is a unit vector perpendicular to the plane of \(\vec{A}\) and \(\vec{B}\).

Right-hand rule

  • Point fingers along \(\vec{A}\).
  • Curl fingers toward \(\vec{B}\) through the smaller angle.
  • Thumb gives the direction of \(\vec{A}\times\vec{B}\).

Key properties

  • Anti-commutative: \(\vec{A}\times\vec{B} = -(\vec{B}\times\vec{A})\)
  • \(\vec{A}\times\vec{B}=\vec{0}\) if vectors are parallel or one is zero
  • Maximum magnitude when \(\theta=90^\circ\)
  • \(\vec{A}\times\vec{B}\) is perpendicular to both \(\vec{A}\) and \(\vec{B}\)

Unit vector cross products

\(\hat{i}\times\hat{j}=\hat{k},\quad \hat{j}\times\hat{k}=\hat{i},\quad \hat{k}\times\hat{i}=\hat{j}\)
\(\hat{j}\times\hat{i}=-\hat{k},\quad \hat{k}\times\hat{j}=-\hat{i},\quad \hat{i}\times\hat{k}=-\hat{j}\)

Component (determinant) form

\[ \vec{A}\times\vec{B}= \begin{vmatrix} \hat{i} & \hat{j} & \hat{k}\\ A_x & A_y & A_z\\ B_x & B_y & B_z \end{vmatrix} \]
